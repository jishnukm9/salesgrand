Account Classification System

1. Assets
Current Assets
  • Accounts Receivable: Amounts owed to the company by customers for goods/services sold on credit. Example: Customer A/R, Trade Receivables
  • Accrued Income: Income earned but not yet received. Example: Interest Accrued, Rent Receivable
  • Advance to Suppliers: Payments made to suppliers before receiving goods/services. Example: Supplier Advance, Contractor Prepayments
  • Bills Receivable: Written promises received from debtors to pay a specified amount. Example: Trade Bills Receivable, Customer Promissory Notes
  • Branch Account: Account maintaining transactions with branch offices. Example: Branch Current Account, Inter-branch Transfers
  • Other Current Assets: Assets expected to be converted to cash within one year. Example: Tax Refunds Receivable, Insurance Claims Receivable
  • Prepaid Expenses: Expenses paid in advance for future benefits. Example: Prepaid Insurance, Prepaid Rent
  • Short-term Investments: Investments expected to be converted to cash within one year. Example: Treasury Bills, Commercial Paper
Non-Current Assets
  • Fixed Assets: Long-term tangible assets not intended for sale. Example: Machinery, Furniture
  • Intangible Assets: Non-physical assets with long-term value. Example: Patents, Trademarks
  • Long-term Investments: Investments held for more than one year. Example: Long-term Bonds, Investment Properties
  • Other Non-current Assets: Other long-term assets not classified elsewhere. Example: Long-term Deposits, Restricted Cash
  • Property, Plant, and Equipment: Tangible assets used in business operations. Example: Buildings, Plant Machinery
2. Liabilities
Current Liabilities
  • Accounts Payable: Amounts owed to suppliers for goods/services received on credit. Example: Trade Payables, Vendor Accounts
  • Accrued Expenses: Expenses incurred but not yet paid. Example: Accrued Salaries, Accrued Interest
  • Advance Received from Customers: Payments received before delivering goods/services. Example: Customer Advances, Project Advances
  • Other Current Liabilities: Other obligations due within one year. Example: Tax Payable, Dividend Payable
  • Other Payables: Miscellaneous amounts owed in short term. Example: Expense Payables, Commission Payable
  • Short-term Loans: Loans payable within one year. Example: Bank Overdraft, Working Capital Loan
Non-Current Liabilities
  • Advances from Directors or Promoters: Long-term loans from company directors. Example: Director's Loan Account, Promoter's Advance
  • Long-term Loans: Loans payable after one year. Example: Term Loans, Debentures
  • Lease Obligations: Long-term lease payment commitments. Example: Finance Lease Liability, Operating Lease Liability
  • Bills Payable: Written promises to pay a specified amount. Example: Long-term Bills, Deferred Payment Notes
  • Other Non-current Liabilities: Other long-term obligations. Example: Security Deposits, Deferred Tax Liability
3. Equity
  • Reserves and Surplus: Accumulated profits and special funds. Example: General Reserve, Capital Reserve
  • Share Capital: Value of shares issued to shareholders. Example: Equity Share Capital, Preference Share Capital
4. Income
Direct Income
  • Sales Account: Revenue from primary business activities. Example: Product Sales, Service Revenue
  • Services Account: Income from services rendered. Example: Consulting Fees, Professional Services
  • Other Direct Income: Other income directly related to operations. Example: Commission Earned, Installation Charges
Indirect Income
  • Financial Income: Income from financial investments. Example: Interest Income, Dividend Income
  • Miscellaneous Income: Income not related to main business. Example: Rent Income, Scrap Sales
  • Other Indirect Income: Other non-operational income. Example: Foreign Exchange Gain, Insurance Claims
5. Expenses
Direct Expenses
  • Purchase Account: Cost of goods purchased for resale. Example: Raw Material Purchases, Trading Goods
  • Salary and Wages: Employee compensation directly related to production. Example: Factory Wages, Production Staff Salary
  • Other Direct Expenses: Other costs directly related to production. Example: Factory Power, Direct Labor
Indirect Expenses
  • Administrative Expenses: Expenses related to general administration. Example: Office Rent, Printing & Stationery
  • Financial Expenses: Costs related to financing activities. Example: Bank Charges, Interest Expense
  • Miscellaneous Expenses: Various other indirect expenses. Example: Insurance, Repairs & Maintenance
  • Other Indirect Expense: Additional overhead costs. Example: Staff Welfare, Training Expenses
  • Selling & Distribution Expenses: Costs related to sales and distribution. Example: Advertisement, Sales Commission

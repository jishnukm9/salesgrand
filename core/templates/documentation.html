Account Heads Classification

Asset Side (Balance Sheet)
  • Branch Accounts: Money given to branches for operations. Example: Head Office giving ₹10,000 to a branch
  • Stock in Hand: Current inventory value. Example: 100 laptops worth ₹50,000
  • Fixed Assets: Long-term assets. Example: Office building worth ₹500,000
  • Investments: Money invested for returns. Example: ₹100,000 in government bonds
  • Loan and Advances: Money to be received back. Example: ₹20,000 employee loan
  • Other Assets: Miscellaneous assets. Example: Prepaid insurance ₹5,000
Liability Side (Balance Sheet)
  • Borrowings: Money owed to others. Example: Bank loan of ₹100,000
  • Deposits: Money held temporarily. Example: Customer security deposit of ₹1,000
  • Other Liabilities: Miscellaneous payables. Example: Unpaid electricity bill ₹500
Equity Side (Balance Sheet)
  • Reserves and Surpluses: Accumulated profits. Example: General reserve of ₹50,000
  • Share Capital: Owner's investment. Example: 1000 shares @ ₹100 each = ₹100,000
Income Side (Profit & Loss)
  • Incomes: Money earned. Example: Interest received ₹1,000
  • Sales Account: Product sales income. Example: Product sales ₹200,000
  • Service Account: Service income. Example: Consulting fees ₹50,000
  • Purchase Return: Reduction in purchases. Example: Returned defective purchases ₹2,000
Expense Side (Profit & Loss)
  • Expenses: Regular costs. Example: Electricity bill ₹1,000
  • Purchase Accounts: Cost of goods. Example: Purchased goods worth ₹150,000
  • Salary and Wages: Employee costs. Example: Monthly staff salaries ₹30,000
  • Sales Return: Reduction in sales. Example: Customer returned goods worth ₹3,000
  • Trade Expenses: Trading costs. Example: Transportation cost ₹5,000
